CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management. It is a software system used by companies to manage customer information, track interactions, improve communication, and increase sales. CRM helps organizations maintain better relationships with customers and manage business activities more efficiently.

## Why Companies Use Salesforce
Salesforce is one of the most popular CRM platforms used by companies around the world. Companies use Salesforce because it helps store customer data in one place, automate business processes, improve teamwork, track leads and opportunities, and generate useful reports. Since Salesforce is cloud-based, it can be accessed from anywhere.

## Salesforce Objects

### Account
An Account represents a company, organization, or institution. Examples include Infosys, TCS, and ABC College.

### Contact
A Contact represents a person associated with an Account. Examples include a student, HR manager, or doctor.

### Opportunity
An Opportunity represents a possible business deal or process that may lead to success. Examples include a college admission process, event sponsorship, or placement partnership.

## Business Flow
The business flow in CRM follows the process Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer.

A Lead is a person who shows interest in a product or service. In a college admission system, a student asking about admission details is considered a lead.
After collecting and verifying the details, the lead becomes a Contact. In this stage, the student submits the application form and personal details.
An Opportunity is created when there is a possibility of converting the contact into a customer. In this case, the admission process is ongoing.
Finally, when the process is completed successfully, the person becomes a Customer. In this example, the student officially joins the college.

## Real-Life Mapping (College Admission System)
Account = College
Contact = Student
Lead = Student interested in admission
Opportunity = Admission process
